DiffFashion

Reference-Based Fashion Design with Structure-Aware Transfer by Diffusion Models

Authors: Shidong Cao; Wenhao Chai; Shengyu Hao; Yanting Zhang; Hangyue Chen; Gaoang Wang

IEEE Transactions on Multimedia (Vol. 26, 2024)

1
MOTIVATION

The Challenge

How can AI generate a new fashion image that keeps clothing structure while borrowing appearance from a reference?

In reference-based fashion design, we want to combine a source clothing image (structure) and a reference appearance image (texture / style) to synthesize a realistic new clothing image.

Existing diffusion-based translation often fails when the reference domain differs from the source domain.

So why do current methods collapse?
2
PROBLEM ANALYSIS RESEARCH GAP

Why Appearance Transfer Collapses

Key issue: During reverse diffusion, it is hard to transfer appearance realistically.

Domain gap between reference appearance and source clothing can cause translation collapse.

What We Need

  • Preserve structure from the source clothing (shape, layout, garment parts)
  • Transfer appearance from the reference image (texture, color, patterns)
  • Maintain semantics in foreground clothing regions
DiffFashion: structure-aware guidance for diffusion.
3
METHOD

DiffFashion: Core Idea

Structure-Aware Transfer in Diffusion

DiffFashion injects mixed guidance into the reverse diffusion process to preserve clothing structure while transferring reference appearance.

Guidance 1: ViT Guidance

Use a pre-trained Vision Transformer to provide robust semantic cues for appearance transfer.

Guidance 2: Foreground Mask

Focus constraints on foreground clothing regions to avoid background leakage and preserve garment layout.

Next: how diffusion is guided step-by-step.
4
METHOD DETAILS

Diffusion Process (High-Level)

Diffusion models define a forward process that gradually adds Gaussian noise, and a reverse process that learns to denoise and generate images.

DiffFashion adds structure-aware constraints during reverse diffusion to stabilize translation under domain gaps.

Guided Reverse Diffusion

  • Semantic guidance from a pre-trained ViT improves appearance realism
  • Foreground masking strengthens structure retention on garment regions
  • Mixed guidance balances structure + appearance throughout denoising
What does “structure-aware transfer” enforce?
5
STRUCTURE-AWARE TRANSFER

How Structure is Preserved

Keep the Source Layout

  • Garment silhouette
  • Part boundaries (collar, sleeves, etc.)
  • Spatial arrangement

Transfer the Reference Look

  • Texture & patterns
  • Color & material cues
  • Appearance semantics

With mixed guidance, the method reduces collapse when the reference appearance domain differs greatly from the source domain, producing more coherent and realistic fashion images.

Now: does it work in experiments?
6
EXPERIMENTS

Results

Better Realism and Fidelity

Experiments show DiffFashion outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in the fashion design task, generating more realistic images while preserving structure and appearance semantics.

What Improves

  • Realism of generated clothing appearance
  • Structural similarity to the source clothing
  • Robustness under large cross-domain differences
So what are the main contributions?
7
CONTRIBUTIONS

What This Paper Contributes

DiffFashion is a diffusion-based framework for reference-based fashion design with structure-aware transfer.

  • Introduces mixed guidance (pre-trained ViT + foreground mask) to preserve structure and transfer appearance
  • Addresses domain-gap-induced collapse in reverse diffusion
  • Generates realistic fashion images with improved structure and semantic fidelity
  • Outperforms state-of-the-art baselines in experiments
Wrap-up: key takeaways.
8

Conclusion

DiffFashion improves reference-based fashion generation by guiding diffusion with structure-aware constraints.

Takeaway: Mixed guidance (ViT + foreground mask) helps diffusion models transfer appearance realistically while preserving clothing structure—especially under large domain gaps.
