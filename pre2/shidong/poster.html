DiffFashion: Reference-based Fashion Design with Structure-aware Transfer by Diffusion Models

Presenter: Shidong Cao

One-sentence takeaway DiffFashion generates new clothing designs by combining the structure of a source garment with the appearance of a reference image, even when the two images come from very different domains.

Background

AI-based fashion design aims to support designers by generating new clothing concepts from visual references. A useful setting is reference-based fashion design: starting from a clothing image and a separate appearance image, generate a realistic new fashion item that keeps the garment shape while borrowing colors, textures, or patterns from the reference.

Diffusion models are attractive for this task because they can generate realistic images and work in a zero-shot setting, without training a new model for every new style.

Research Problem

Existing image translation and style transfer methods struggle in this setting for two main reasons.

Large domain gap A clothing image and a reference image such as marine life or natural objects can be semantically very different, so appearance transfer often collapses or looks unrealistic.
No paired training data Newly designed target fashions do not have ground-truth samples, so supervised training is not practical and structure is easily lost during generation.

Method

DiffFashion uses a diffusion-based, unsupervised structure-aware transfer pipeline.

1. Shared latent transfer The method starts from the reference appearance image and keeps a shared DDPM latent so appearance information can gradually adapt into the clothing domain.
2. ViT mixed guidance DINO-ViT features guide both structure and appearance: local patch features preserve garment layout, while global features preserve reference semantics.
3. Automatic mask guidance A foreground mask is generated from diffusion noise differences, then used to keep clothing structure without requiring manual annotations.

Pipeline summary: reference image provides appearance, source clothing image provides structure, and the diffusion process combines them into a realistic new fashion output.

Key Contributions

  • Introduces DiffFashion, an unsupervised diffusion framework for reference-based fashion design.
  • Proposes a shared-latent transfer strategy motivated by optimal transport properties to handle large source/reference domain gaps.
  • Combines ViT appearance and structure guidance with automatic foreground mask guidance for better realism and garment preservation.
  • Builds the OceanBag benchmark with 6,000 handbag images and 2,400 marine-life reference images, and validates the method on other clothing categories.

Results

DiffFashion is compared with DiffuseIT, Splice, WCT2, and SANet on OceanBag and other clothing datasets. The method achieves the best overall balance between realism, structure preservation, and appearance transfer.

User Study Overall 73.6 Best score on OceanBag
Appearance Score 49.4 Best subjective appearance transfer
LPIPS (Appearance) 0.754 Best objective appearance similarity
FID 131.8 Second-best realism, while preserving transfer
  • DiffuseIT preserves shape reasonably well but transfers too little appearance.
  • Splice transfers appearance better but often produces unrealistic outputs.
  • WCT2 obtains strong realism and structure scores, but mainly changes color rather than generating new fashion textures.
  • DiffFashion provides the strongest overall trade-off, especially when the reference image comes from a very different domain.

Ablation results also show that mask guidance and ViT structure guidance are important for preserving garment structure.

Conclusion

DiffFashion addresses a difficult but practical design problem: generating new clothing from a garment image and an unrelated visual reference without paired training data. Its shared-latent transfer and mixed-guidance design allow realistic fashion synthesis across large domain gaps.

The main limitation is mask quality: because masks are generated from diffusion behavior, some cases still require selection or tuning. Future work will improve mask generation with richer conditions or additional modalities.
